Kenjalgad Fort


Overview

Kenjalgad Fort, also known as Ghera Khelanja, is located in the Wai taluka of Maharashtraâ€™s Satara district. Perched atop a hill in the Western Ghats, it offers a scenic trekking experience with breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys. Built during the time of the Marathas, the fort served as a watchtower to monitor nearby regions.

History

Kenjalgad Fort has a long history that dates back to the Satavahana era, but it gained prominence during the rule of the Marathas and later the British. It was strategically important due to its location overlooking the Mahabaleshwar range. The British captured it in 1818. Today, it stands as a reminder of the region's historical importance.

Difficulty Level and Useful Tips

The trek to Kenjalgad is moderate and can be completed in about 1.5-2 hours. The trail is steep in some sections, so proper trekking shoes and sufficient water are recommended. The best time to visit is during the monsoon or winter seasons when the landscape is lush and green.

What to See on the Fort?

  • Ruins of the Fort Walls
  • Rock-cut Water Cisterns
  • Mesmerizing views of the Mahabaleshwar plateau
  • View of nearby forts like Kamalgad and Pandavgad
