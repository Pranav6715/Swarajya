Tikona Fort


Overview

Tikona Fort, also known as Vitandgad, is located in the Maval region of Maharashtra, near Lonavala. Famous for its triangular shape (hence the name Tikona), the fort offers panoramic views of the Pavana dam and surrounding valleys. It's an ideal destination for trekking enthusiasts and history lovers.

History

Tikona Fort has a rich history that is linked to the Maratha empire. It was conquered by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in the 1650s, and the fort played a strategic role in the Maratha kingdom's defense. Later, it was taken over by the British in 1818. The fort remains a popular trekking destination for history buffs and nature lovers alike.

Difficulty Level and Useful Tips

The trek to Tikona Fort is easy to moderate and can be completed in about 1-1.5 hours. The trail is steep but manageable, making it suitable for beginners.
