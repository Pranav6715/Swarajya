Malhargad


Overview

Malhargad Fort, also known as Sonori Fort, is located near Saswad in the Pune district of Maharashtra, India. Built in the 18th century, it is one of the last forts constructed by the Maratha Empire. The fort is named after the deity Malhari (a form of Lord Shiva) and offers a scenic view of the surrounding area.

History

Malhargad was constructed between 1757-1760 by Sardar Bhivrao Yashwant. The fort was built to oversee the nearby Dive Ghat and monitor the Nira and Karha river valleys. Though it did not play a major role in battles, it is considered important as one of the final constructions of the Maratha period.

Difficulty Level and Useful Tips

The trek to Malhargad is easy and can be completed in about 1-2 hours. The trail is well-marked and suitable for beginners. Make sure to carry sufficient water and snacks, as there are no facilities at the top. It's a perfect place for a peaceful day trip with family or friends.

What to See on the Fort?

  • Malhargad Temple (dedicated to Lord Shiva)
  • Two Water Tanks (ancient structures)
  • Ruins of the fort walls
  • View of Purandar Fort and Dive Ghat
