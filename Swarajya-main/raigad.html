Raigad Fort


Overview

Raigad Fort, located in the Raigad district of Maharashtra, is known for its historical significance as the capital of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj's empire. The fort offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and is a popular destination for trekkers and history enthusiasts.

History

Raigad Fort was built in the 17th century and served as the capital of the Maratha Empire. It is famous for its architectural marvels and the crowning ceremony of Shivaji Maharaj as the king of the Maratha Empire took place here.

Difficulty Level and Useful Tips

The trek to Raigad Fort is moderately challenging, typically taking around 1.5 to 2.5 hours. It is advisable to wear sturdy shoes, carry sufficient water, and start early in the morning to avoid the heat.

What to See on the Fort?

  • The impressive entrance gate (Palkhi Darwaza)
  • The famous samadhi (memorial) of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj
  • The majestic Raigad Fort palace ruins
  • Stunning views from the fort's highest points
  • The breathtaking sunset view from the fort
